Methodology & Calibration Disclosures (Proxy Model)

Why is this a Proxy Model?
The enforcement agency's violation dataset does not contain real-world vehicle travel times, speeds, or delays. To prevent losing credibility by overclaiming precision, this scoring layer uses OpenStreetMap road geometry, lane capacities, and transit proximity to model the potential traffic-flow impact of illegal parking.

Scoring Formula:
Congestion Impact = Density × Narrowness Penalty × (1 + Peak Hour Overlap) × (1 + POI Proximity Score)

  • Violation Density: Normalized parking violation frequency per grid cell (56m resolution).
  • Narrowness Penalty: Scaled inverse of road capacity (derived from OSM tags: highway_type × lanes). Narrower streets carry a higher penalty.
  • Peak Hour Overlap: Share of violations logged during observed daytime traffic rush hours (8-11 AM and 5-8 PM IST).
  • POI Proximity: Distance-decay sum of nearby metro stations, bus stops/stations, and markets within 300m.
v2 Roadmap (Real-World Calibration):

To transition this model from a proxy score to a validated traffic-flow index, the next product iteration requires:

  1. Google Maps Directions API: Querying historical travel times along snapped road segments during peak vs. off-peak hours to measure actual speed deltas.
  2. Live Traffic Layers: Correlating vehicle densities with live traffic speeds near active hotspots.
  3. Sensory Calibration: Snapping camera logs to road-side induction loops or microwave radar vehicle counters.
